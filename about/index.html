About · عن الكامب

“Rayga” is a state of mind.

Ask anyone on the promenade what الرايجه means and they'll shrug, smile, and point at the sea. It's Dahab slang for that unhurried, salt-cured calm you get after a few days here. We named the camp after it because that's the whole product.

01 — The place

A courtyard on Peace Road

El Rayga sits in Masbat, the old heart of Dahab, one row back from the water. Palm-wood huts and simple rooms wrap around a green inner courtyard — bougainvillea, hammocks, a fire pit, and the Ugarit Syria kitchen serving until 2 AM. Alaska Camp and the Dahab Volunteers house are our neighbours; the lagoon is a two-minute barefoot walk.

02 — The idea

Camps came first

Before the hotels, Dahab was camps: palm walls, mattresses, tea, and the reef. Most of them are gone or gentrified. We keep the format and fix the parts that deserved fixing — real mattresses, cold AC where it counts, bathrooms cleaned twice a day, Wi-Fi that reaches the hammocks. Simple isn't the same as sloppy.

03 — The people

Ask for Dawod

Guests keep writing it, so we'll say it: Dawod at the front desk is the spirit of the camp — a recent Google review called him exactly that. The desk runs 24 hours, books your dives and safaris, and remembers how you take your tea by day two. Solo travellers arrive alone and leave with a group photo.

04 — The honesty bit

We're a camp, not a resort

Huts have shared bathrooms. The desert has mosquitoes at dusk. Music from the promenade drifts in on Fridays. When we get a rough review — and we have — we read it at the desk and fix what's fixable. The rest is camp life, and it's the point. Read every review, good and bad, on the reviews page.
